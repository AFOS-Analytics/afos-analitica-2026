## 1. Prediction market

[Polymarket](https://polymarket.com/politics/brazil) closed April 23 with the presidential race **inverted relative to the previous night**. **Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva (PT)** held at 38.5%, while **Flávio Bolsonaro (PL)** retreated from 38.95% (Apr 22) to 37.95% — in two consecutive moves: a drop to 37.75% in the morning, partial recovery of 0.2pp in the afternoon. The gap closed the day at **+0.55pp in favor of Lula**, against -0.45pp in favor of Flávio 24 hours earlier.

The day's outright headline, however, belonged to **Romeu Zema (Novo)**. In the "third place in [1º turno](/en/glossary#primeiro-turno)" market, the governor jumped from 29% in the morning to **33% at night (+4pp in 8 hours)**, while Renan Santos rose slightly from 27% to 28.5%. The gap between them, which was 2pp in the morning and 0.5pp the day before, **widened to 4.5pp**. In the presidential market, however, Zema gave back part of the gain: closed at 6.95% (-0.3pp). Renan ceded to 5.65% (-0.2pp).

The most unexpected move appeared in the "second place in [1º turno](/en/glossary#primeiro-turno)" market. **Lula exploded** from 14.5% (Apr 22) to **22%** — a 7.5pp jump unprecedented in the cycle — and held at that level throughout the day. Flávio, in the same market, retreated from 64.5% to **59.5%** (-5pp cumulative). **Fernando Haddad** oscillated short-term: fell from 6.1% to 4.55% in the morning and recovered to 5.3% at night.

[STF](/en/glossary#stf) justice impeachment closed the session at **12.5%** — stable relative to the morning, but three consecutive sessions of gains took it from 11% (Apr 22 morning) to this level. The cycle peak remains **13.5%**, on April 21. In the Senate, [PL](/en/glossary#pl) held 82%, but **the centrão recovered**: [PSD](/en/glossary#psd) rose from 4.7% to **5.4%** (+0.7pp) and MDB jumped from 3.75% to **5.25%** (+1.5pp). [PT](/en/glossary#pt) held the 3.6% reached in the morning.

In 2026 inflation expectations, the 5.00-5.49% band leads with **40.2%**, but retreated 1.15pp on the day. The 4.50-4.99% band rose to 37.1% (+1pp) and 3.50-3.99% gained 1.1pp. Overall, the market priced **slightly lower inflation** than the day before.

## 2. What the institutes registered

The day's headline at the [TSE](/en/glossary#tse) was the formal registration of the **new AtlasIntel presidential poll**, with field April 22-27 and a sample of **5,000 respondents** — the largest of the cycle so far — published by [CartaCapital](https://www.cartacapital.com.br) at 15:42. Also entered into the system was the **first [Quaest](/en/glossary#quaest) poll for the Minas Gerais governor's race**, confirmed by [VEJA](https://veja.abril.com.br).

A new national divergence emerged: the **Quaest released by MSN** this Thursday recorded **Flávio ahead of Lula in the runoff, 42% to 40%** — contradicting the [CNT](https://www.cnt.org.br) of April 21, which had given Lula the lead with 44% to 40%. In Teresina, [Piauí Hoje](https://www.piauihoje.com) showed Lula at **68%**, widening the gap.

The week's calendar densified further. In addition to **April 27** (three Quaest, [Paraná Pesquisas](/en/glossary#parana-pesquisas) second round, and the first [Nexus](/en/glossary#nexus-btg) — 6,704 respondents), now **April 28** concentrates the AtlasIntel of 5,000, two additional Quaest, Instituto Novo Perfil, Data AZ, and Exatus. A sum of about **9,800 respondents** in a single day.
