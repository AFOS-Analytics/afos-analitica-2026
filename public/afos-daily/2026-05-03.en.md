## 1. What the prediction market priced

In the [Brazil Presidential Election](https://polymarket.com/event/brazil-presidential-election) market, Flávio Bolsonaro closed the day at **43.95%** (↑1.30pp from May 2), reversing the drop from the top registered yesterday. Lula fell to **37.50%** (↓1.0pp), reversing the recovery made on Monday. The gap between the two widened again to **+6.45pp**, against +4.15pp in the previous close.

The technical reading is a return to last week's pattern after the oscillation on May 1 and 2: the market oscillated around 42-44% for Flávio and 37-39% for Lula, with the gap varying between 4 and 6.5pp. In the [2nd place](https://polymarket.com/event/brazil-presidential-election-first-round-2nd-place) market, Flávio maintains **67.5%** (↓0.5pp slight) and Lula **19.0%** (↓0.5pp slight) — both with small reversals, without structural change.

In the third way, [Renan Santos](https://polymarket.com/event/brazil-presidential-election-first-round-3rd-place) **surges to 36% in the 3rd place market** (↑2.5pp, from 33.5%), reducing the gap with Romeu Zema, who maintains the lead at **39.5%** (↑0.50pp slight). It is the first significant convergence in recent weeks. In the presidential race, Renan remains stable at **5.65%** (↑0.05pp) and Zema continues plummeting, closing at **3.75%** (↓0.40pp), fourth consecutive day below 5%. [Eduardo Leite](https://polymarket.com/event/brazil-presidential-election-first-round-3rd-place) reversed strongly in 3rd place to 0.85% (↓0.65pp, from 1.5%).

Haddad plummets for the second consecutive day in the presidential race, closing at **2.95%** (↓0.70pp, from 3.65%). Camilo Santana remains above him at 3.85% (↓0.10pp slight). Fragmentation on the left worsens: Camilo is 0.90pp ahead of Haddad, against a tie at the beginning of the week.

In the [STF impeach](https://polymarket.com/event/any-brazil-stf-justice-removed-by-impeachment-before-2027), the market reversed to **13.5%** (↓0.5pp slight, from 14%). In the [Senate](https://polymarket.com/event/next-brazil-senate-election-most-seats-won), reverse reorganization of yesterday's movement: [PL](/en/glossary#pl) widens massively to **87%** (↑7.5pp, from 79.5%), almost certain consolidation; União recovers slightly to 5.5% (↑0.9pp); [Novo](/en/glossary#novo) reverses strongly to 4.5% (↓3.4pp); [PSD](/en/glossary#psd) plummets to 2.15% (↓5.35pp); MDB plummets to 1.25% (↓3.3pp). In [2026 inflation](https://polymarket.com/event/brazil-annual-inflation-2026), all 6%+ bands surge for the second consecutive day: 6.00-6.49% to 16.15% (↑6.3pp), 6.50-6.99% to 16.05% (↑4.45pp), 7%+ to 11.4% (↑3.25pp).

## 2. What the polling institutes registered

Two national polls released today. **Nexus 2T** ([Capital News](https://capitalnews.com.br)) registers a [technical tie](/en/glossary#empate-tecnico) of Lula with Flávio, Zema, and Caiado in all four runoff scenarios — four competitive opponents in the [2º turno](/en/glossary#segundo-turno). **VOX BRASIL SP 2T** ([Folha do Estado](https://www.folhadoestado.com.br)) shows Flávio 50.4% × Lula 38.1% in São Paulo, with Lula losing by more than 12pp in the country's largest electoral college.

[Real Time Big Data PA](https://www.cnnbrasil.com.br) (released May 2, maintained) confirms Lula leading all [1º turno](/en/glossary#primeiro-turno) scenarios in Pará. [Quaest](/en/glossary#quaest) released a survey in 6 of 10 states where independent governors are preferred by voters.

## 4. Divergences of the day

> **[Polymarket](/en/glossary#polymarket) × Wellington Dias (Lula campaign):** The campaign coordinator stated today that Flávio "has hit the ceiling of the polls" ([Brasil 247](https://www.brasil247.com), [VEJA](https://veja.abril.com.br)). [Polymarket](/en/glossary#polymarket) priced exactly the opposite movement — Flávio recovered 1.30pp and the gap widened from 4.15pp to 6.45pp. The divergence does not mean that one side is wrong: the coordinator speaks of traditional polls; [Polymarket](/en/glossary#polymarket) integrates bets with real money. But the mismatch between narrative and pricing is direct.

> **SP polls × National [Polymarket](/en/glossary#polymarket) × government narrative:** [VOX BRASIL](https://www.folhadoestado.com.br) shows Flávio 50.4% × Lula 38.1% in SP in the [2º turno](/en/glossary#segundo-turno) (12.3pp difference). [National Polymarket](/en/glossary#polymarket) shows Flávio as favorite (43.95% × 37.50%). But [Nexus 2T](https://capitalnews.com.br) registers a [technical tie](/en/glossary#empate-tecnico) of Lula with Flávio, Zema, and Caiado in all four national scenarios. The three signals — SP poll (strong anti-Lula), national [Polymarket](/en/glossary#polymarket) (moderate anti-Lula), Nexus 2T ([technical tie](/en/glossary#empate-tecnico) in all scenarios) — are not contradictory, but show that the reading "everything decided" on one side and "everything open" on the other does not fit.

> **Senate consolidation × fragmented state-level:** [PL](/en/glossary#pl) widens to 87% in the national [Senate](https://polymarket.com/event/next-brazil-senate-election-most-seats-won) (almost certain consolidation for the right). But [Quaest](/en/glossary#quaest) shows that in 6 of 10 states voters prefer independent governors — an anti-establishment signal that benefits both non-Bolsonarist center-right and Renan/MBL. The window for a third way in the [presidential race](https://polymarket.com/event/brazil-presidential-election) remains closed (Zema 4th day <5%, Renan 5.65% stable), but the state-level window remains open.
