## What forkers and derivative projects CAN do

- **Reference AFOS Analytics as the upstream project by name** in your README, documentation, and NOTICE file. This is the Apache 2.0 attribution requirement — it is allowed and encouraged.
- **Describe your project as a derivative**: "a fork of AFOS Analytics", "derived from AFOS Analytics", or "based on the AFOS Analytics codebase".
- **Link** to [https://afos-analytics.com](https://afos-analytics.com) and [https://github.com/AFOS-Analytics/afos-analitica-2026](https://github.com/AFOS-Analytics/afos-analitica-2026) as the source.
- **Operate your fork under a different name** of your choice.
- **Use the AFOS name in comparison contexts**, provided the comparison is factual ("compared to AFOS Analytics...", "AFOS-compatible...").

## What forkers CANNOT do

- **Name your project or product** "AFOS", "AFOS Analytics", or any name likely to create confusion with the official AFOS Analytics — this includes names like "AFOS Pro", "AFOS Plus", "AFOS Enterprise", "MyAFOS", "AFOS.io", or similar permutations.
- **Use the AFOS Analytics logo, favicon, visual identity, color palette-as-identity**, or any adaptation of these.
- **Imply endorsement, sponsorship, or official affiliation** with AFOS Analytics, its maintainers, or the AFOS Analytics project.
- **Claim your fork is the "official"** AFOS Analytics, "AFOS 2.0", or any suggestion of successor status.
- **Register domains, social handles, or trademarks** that include "AFOS" in a way that could mislead users into thinking your project is the original.
- **Use AFOS marks in a way that disparages** the AFOS Analytics project or its community.

## Required disclaimer for commercial or public-facing forks

If you operate a fork publicly (commercial or not — any publicly accessible instance), your user-facing materials (README, website, marketing, product UI) must include a clear disclaimer such as:

> **"[Your Project Name]"** is a fork of [AFOS Analytics](https://afos-analytics.com), licensed under Apache 2.0. This project is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by AFOS Analytics.

This disclaimer must appear in at least one prominent user-facing location — README, about page, or site footer.

## Why this policy exists

The AFOS Analytics code is free by design — anyone can fork, modify, and operate their own instance. That is the Apache 2.0 promise and AFOS honors it without reservation.

The AFOS Analytics **name and brand**, however, carry editorial responsibility. When readers see "AFOS" attached to an analysis, they expect our integrity rules, our validators, our data pipeline, our governance. A fork that uses the AFOS name — whether well-intentioned or not — borrows that expectation without delivering on it. This policy prevents that confusion.

**The same approach is used by virtually every serious open-source project with a brand**: PostgreSQL, Linux, React, Kubernetes, Elasticsearch, MongoDB, Red Hat. Free code, protected brand.

---

## What this policy is NOT

- It is **not** a license restriction. You can still do everything Apache 2.0 allows with the code — fork, modify, redistribute, use commercially, charge for services.
- It is **not** a non-compete clause. Nothing here prevents you from competing with AFOS Analytics using a fork, as long as you do not use our name or brand.
- It is **not** negotiable for individual forkers. Enforcement is consistent — the policy applies to everyone equally.
